About the Instructor

Sebastian S. James is Professor of Public Policy at the Sanford School of Public Policy at Duke University. He previously served as a Senior Economist at the World Bank, where he worked for over fifteen years. At the World Bank, he advised governments across Africa, Asia, Latin America, the Middle East, and Eastern Europe on tax policy and tax administration, with a focus on economic growth and fiscal sustainability. His work also spans the intersection of tax policy with investment, inequality, and climate change, and his research on tax incentives has been widely influential. Prior to joining the World Bank, he was a member of the Indian Revenue Service, where he held senior roles in tax administration and policy from 1996 to 2002.
